Accessibility

Alt text describes intent, not visuals. "No articles scored yet" — not "A purple lighthouse with a yellow beam." For decorative inline illustrations, use alt="" so screen readers skip them.

Resizable panel

16 px hit area (4 px outside the visible edge, 12 px inside). Edge highlights to 06-primary on hover/pressed; cursor switches to col-resize.

Reorderable items

Drag initiates only from the dedicated handle (never the row body). grab on hover, grabbing mid-drag. A 2 px 06-primary insertion line follows the cursor between candidate drop targets.

Interactive icons

Decorative icons aren't focusable. Tooltip-trigger icons are focusable and must show the tooltip on both hover and focus (WCAG parity). 24 × 24 minimum hit area, even when the visible icon is 16 px — pad the hit area, don't scale the icon.

Esc always cancels

In-progress drag, open tooltip, active resize, modal — Esc returns the user to a known-good state without committing the change.

Spark Cards

Cards are versatile containers used to group related content and actions in a visually distinct block. Every card is either a Display Card (an inert container with interactive children) or an Interactive Card (a selectable unit, checkbox-only inside), in one of two orientations — Vertical or Horizontal. This demo applies every rule in spark-cards/SKILL.md using tokens from spark-colors, spark-typography, spark-spacing-radius, spark-shadows, and composes spark-buttons, spark-icons, and spark-input-fields wherever an inner element ships from another skill.

01 · Card types

Display Card vs Interactive Card

The single most important decision in this skill. Pick by asking: "Does the user click the card, or click something inside it?"

Display Card · non-interactive

A container that presents information. The container itself is inert — but it may contain interactive elements (buttons, links, kebab menus) that each carry their own hover/focus/pressed states.
